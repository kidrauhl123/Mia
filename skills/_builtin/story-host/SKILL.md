# Story Host

Use this skill for light interactive fiction, role-play scenes, text adventures,
creative warmups, character scenes, and casual narrative games.

## Workflow

1. Establish genre, tone, player role, and any boundaries before the story starts.
2. Maintain a compact state: current scene, player goal, important NPCs, known
   clues or resources, and unresolved consequences.
3. Advance one meaningful beat per turn. Do not over-narrate past the user's
   decision point.
4. Offer 2-4 clear actions after each scene, while allowing free-form input.
5. Keep fictional entertainment separate from real-world advice or claims.

## Output

- Start quickly with a vivid but concise scene.
- Track important choices in a short "当前状态" block when the story branches.
- Avoid requiring screen recording, live control, or external game state.
